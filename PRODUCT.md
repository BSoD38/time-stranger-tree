# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are **planners and completionists** playing *Digimon Story: Time Stranger* — players working toward a specific Digimon who need the exact evolution route to it and every requirement along the way. Their context is second-screen: the game is running, and they've come here to answer "I have this, I want that — how do I get there?"

The secondary audience is **casual players mid-playthrough** doing quick single-Digimon lookups — checking one creature's stats, evolutions, or how to obtain it, without needing the full planning apparatus.

## Product Purpose

An interactive explorer for the complete 475-Digimon evolution network of *Digimon Story: Time Stranger*: a living graph of the whole network, a step-by-step route planner that includes de-digivolutions and surfaces every requirement (stats, items, Jogress/DNA partners, agent rank), and a sortable, filterable field guide of the full roster. Success is a player going from "I have X, I want Y" to a complete, actionable plan in seconds — and being able to look up any Digimon's data without friction. It is a fan project; all game content belongs to Bandai Namco.

## Positioning

The complete living graph: the entire evolution network, interactive and true to the game — cycles, de-digivolutions, and all — rather than a static evolution list or a spreadsheet. The interface takes on the signature color of whichever Digimon you're looking at, so the tool feels like part of the world it describes.

## Brand Personality

A living digital-world instrument. The chrome is a quiet graphite / off-white stage that deliberately steps back so each Digimon's own color can lead; the feel is precise and lightly sci-fi — Digivice / terminal energy without cosplaying it. The voice is knowledgeable and direct, a capable field tool rather than a hype site or a cutesy fan page. In three words: precise, alive, subject-driven.

## Anti-references

Generic AI-slop SaaS — cream/beige backgrounds, gradient text, identical icon-card grids, tracked-uppercase eyebrows over every section, the hero-metric template. Cluttered fan-wikis — ad-heavy, dense tables with no hierarchy, inconsistent styling. Bare spreadsheet dumps — a raw data grid with no craft, identity, or interactivity. The app should never read as any of these.

## Design Principles

The subject leads, the chrome recedes. Identity comes from the content — the Digimon and its color — not from decoration applied to the frame.

Answer the whole question. A route is not a list of evolutions; it is every step, de-digivolutions included, with every requirement surfaced. The tool does the arithmetic the player would otherwise do by hand.

Complete and truthful to the data. All 475 Digimon and the real bidirected network, cycles and all, gated by data invariants — never a partial or prettified model of the game.

Craft over category convention. Reject the fan-wiki, the spreadsheet, and the SaaS template. Controls earn familiarity; the whole earns an identity.

Never rely on color alone. Color is one channel; names, markers, and labels always carry the same meaning alongside it — for attributes, resistances, and route-step kinds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for text and UI contrast. `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored throughout — transitions and animations collapse to a crossfade or an instant change. Color is never the sole channel: attribute, resistance, and route-step meaning are always carried by names, markers, or labels as well as by hue.
